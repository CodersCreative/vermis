## The Problem
In Zimbabwe, infestations of Fall Armyworm (FAW) result in annual maize yield losses estimated at approximately USD 83 million,
with affected households exhibiting a 12% higher likelihood of experiencing food insecurity.
Project VERMIS (Versatile Electronic Robot for Managed Insecticide Spraying) proposes a cost-effective,
autonomous robotic solution designed to mitigate this challenge through precision agriculture.

## The Project

<CardGrid stagger>
	<Card title="The Rover" icon="pencil">
		The developed rover integrates embedded artificial intelligence to enable early detection and targeted eradication of FAW populations
		before significant crop damage occurs.
		The system autonomously navigates between crop rows and employs a You Only Look Once (YOLOv11) computer vision model,
		deployed on a Raspberry Pi 5,
		which was trained on a dataset of 300 hyper-localized images to achieve real-time pest identification under field conditions.
	</Card>
	<Card title="The Improvements" icon="pencil">
		To enhance mechanical reliability and operational efficiency,
		the spraying subsystem was optimized by transitioning from a suction-based mechanism to a gravity-fed delivery system.
		This design leverages a hydrostatic pressure of approximately 3004.3 Pa,
		thereby eliminating suction lift limitations and ensuring consistent pesticide flow.	
	</Card>
	<Card title="The Experiments" icon="add-document">
		Experimental evaluation was conducted to compare the efficacy of the rover against conventional knapsack spraying methods.
		Results indicate that the rover achieves an 80% pest reduction per unit of pesticide applied,
		compared to 50% achieved by the knapsack sprayer.
		This demonstrates a substantial improvement in pesticide use efficiency and pest control effectiveness.	
	</Card>
	<Card title="The Results" icon="setting">
		Overall, the proposed system offers a scalable, low-cost, and labor-efficient solution for FAW management.
		By reducing pesticide wastage, minimizing human labor requirements, and improving crop protection outcomes,
		Project VERMIS presents a viable technological intervention for enhancing agricultural productivity and food security in FAW-affected regions.	
	</Card>
</CardGrid>
